### 3. Three "Winning" Visualizations (Copy-Paste Ready)
Judges love these three specific chart types because they show "Analytic Depth." Have these snippets ready to adapt.

#### A. The "Correlation" Chart (Dual Axis)
**Use when:** They ask "Does Weather affect Sales?" or "Do Discounts drive Volume?"
*Why it wins:* It proves a relationship between two different metrics.

```python
# In app/dashboard.py
from plotly.subplots import make_subplots

# Data: date, sales, temperature
fig = make_subplots(specs=[[{"secondary_y": True}]])

# Bar: The Core Metric (e.g., Sales)
fig.add_trace(
    go.Bar(x=df['date'], y=df['sales'], name="Sales", marker_color=COLORS['primary'], opacity=0.6),
    secondary_y=False
)

# Line: The External Factor (e.g., Temperature/Inflation)
fig.add_trace(
    go.Scatter(x=df['date'], y=df['temperature'], name="Temp", line=dict(color=COLORS['secondary'], width=3)),
    secondary_y=True
)

fig.update_layout(title="Impact of Temperature on Sales")
st.plotly_chart(fig, use_container_width=True)
```

#### B. The "Heatmap" (Seasonality)
**Use when:** They ask "When is the busiest time?" (Day of week vs Hour, or Month vs Year).
*Why it wins:* It creates an instant visual pattern that humans recognize immediately.

```python
# In app/dashboard.py
import plotly.express as px

# Data: year, month, revenue
fig = px.density_heatmap(
    df, 
    x="month", 
    y="year", 
    z="revenue", 
    color_continuous_scale="Tealgrn", # Matches your theme
    title="Revenue Intensity Heatmap"
)
st.plotly_chart(fig, use_container_width=True)
```

#### C. The "Dynamic Insight" (Automated Text)
**Use when:** You want to look smarter than everyone else.
*Why it wins:* Instead of forcing the user to read the chart, **you tell them what it says**.

```python
# In app/dashboard.py

# Calculate the fact
best_category = df.loc[df['revenue'].idxmax()]['category']

# Display it as a "News Headline"

""", unsafe_allow_html=True)
```

### 4. Cheat Sheet for "Storytelling"
When you present, do not say: *"Here is a bar chart of sales."*
Say: *"We wanted to know where our revenue is coming from. This chart reveals that..."*

**The "Why -> What -> So What" Framework:**
1.  **Why:** "We noticed revenue was dipping in Q3."
2.  **What:** "We drilled down into the data (show Drill-Down Page) and found it was specifically the 'Snacks' category."
3.  **So What:** "This correlates with the end of tourist season. We propose a 'Local Discount' in September to offset this."
